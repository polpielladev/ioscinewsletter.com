### [⚠️ GitHub Actions macos-latest image is changing](https://github.blog/changelog/2026-05-14-github-actions-upcoming-image-migrations?utm_source=ioscinewsletter&utm_medium=web&utm_campaign=issue_88)

Starting **June 15**, GitHub Actions will migrate `macos-latest` from **macOS 15 to macOS 26** over about **30 days**. This means that your workflows may switch images without any YAML changes on your side, and your builds may start failing.

If you are not ready yet, **pin `macos-15`** until you have tested the upgrade. [Read the changelog](https://github.blog/changelog/2026-05-14-github-actions-upcoming-image-migrations?utm_source=ioscinewsletter&utm_medium=web&utm_campaign=issue_88) for dates and other runner migrations.

### [🔧 Get started with Bazel persistent workers](https://adincebic.com/2026/05/10/a-practical-introduction-to-bazel.html?utm_source=ioscinewsletter&utm_medium=web&utm_campaign=issue_88)

**Persistent workers** keep a long-lived Bazel process alive to avoid repeated JVM or compiler startup costs — useful when you author custom rules and care about build performance.

[Adin Ćebić](https://x.com/adincebic) demonstrates this with a simple **uppercase-file** rule and a Swift worker using Bazel’s JSON protocol. [Their guide](https://adincebic.com/2026/05/10/a-practical-introduction-to-bazel.html?utm_source=ioscinewsletter&utm_medium=web&utm_campaign=issue_88) walks through the full implementation step by step.
